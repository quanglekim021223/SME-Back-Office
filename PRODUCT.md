# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SME owners, finance administrators, bookkeepers, external accountants, and operations managers use SME Back-Office Copilot while handling invoices, bank statements, reconciliation exceptions, and weekly cashflow review. They are usually in a task-oriented finance workflow, not browsing a marketing site.

## Product Purpose

SME Back-Office Copilot is a human-supervised financial operations workspace for uploading source documents, producing traceable AI proposals, routing uncertain work to review, and showing grounded business signals. Success means the user can understand cash movement, unresolved work, and evidence-backed next actions without trusting opaque automation.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, accountable. The product should feel like a premium finance SaaS console: compact, trustworthy, operational, and professional rather than playful or experimental.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI dashboards with loud purple/blue gradients, oversized hero metrics, emoji icons, decorative glassmorphism, fake product screenshots, and marketing copy that promises autonomous finance magic. Avoid consumer fintech flashiness, crypto-style neon, and cluttered enterprise admin screens.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize the next financial action over decorative storytelling.
2. Make uncertainty visible through review counts, status labels, evidence, and confidence language.
3. Keep dense information scannable with compact rows, clear hierarchy, and consistent controls.
4. Use restrained color: one primary accent for action, semantic colors for risk and state.
5. Preserve trust by making mock/local data obvious until real integrations are wired.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, reduced-motion behavior, clear labels, and responsive layouts down to small laptop and mobile widths. Do not rely on color alone for financial status or review priority.
